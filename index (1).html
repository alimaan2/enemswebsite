About ENEMS

An innovation lab, not a casual club

Unlike traditional student organizations, ENEMS functions as a working innovation lab. Members engage in structured problem identification, evidence review, prototyping, validation, and publication.

Every project is expected to move beyond ideation and toward measurable implementation impact. We bridge engineering and clinical execution — developing technically rigorous solutions that hold up in the real world.

ENEMS sits between a medical device startup, a clinical research lab, and a systems engineering unit — all within a student-led framework.

How We Work
  • Research-informed decision making
  • Iterative development and prototyping
  • Systems-level thinking
  • Cross-disciplinary collaboration
  • Measurable validation
  • Publication and knowledge dissemination
Engineering Division

Devices Team

Builds medical solutions from the ground up. Projects begin with a defined operational gap in prehospital care.

Members conduct structured literature reviews, evaluate current standards, generate engineering concepts, and develop iterative prototypes. Emphasis is placed on real-world constraints — workflow integration, field durability, usability, and scalability.

The goal is not concept design, but functional, testable systems ready for deployment pathways.

Prototyping Device Design Literature Review Validation Deployment Field Durability
Research Division

Systems Team

Focuses on identifying inefficiencies and structural gaps within EMS environments.

Work includes workflow optimization, clinical protocol evaluation, systems-level research, and meta-analysis. The team produces structured analyses, performance evaluations, and publishable findings.

Their work strengthens evidence-based implementation and supports broader institutional or policy-level improvements.

Systems Analysis Meta-Analysis Protocol Review Workflow Optimization Publication Policy
Culture & Expectations
